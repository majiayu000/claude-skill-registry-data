# Speech Writer

Craft powerful speeches that move audiences to think, feel, and act. This skill helps you write memorable presentations for any occasionâ€”from keynote addresses and business presentations to wedding toasts and commencement speeches.

Whether you're speaking to inspire, persuade, inform, or celebrate, this skill ensures your speech has a clear structure, compelling narrative, and memorable moments that resonate long after the applause fades. It balances rhetorical techniques with authentic voice to create speeches that sound natural when delivered.

Perfect for executives, thought leaders, educators, event speakers, and anyone who needs to deliver a speech that makes an impact and stays with the audience.

## Core Workflows

### Workflow 1: Keynote Address
1. **Audience Analysis** - Understand who they are, what they care about, why they're here
2. **Core Message** - Identify the one thing audience must remember
3. **Opening Hook** - Create memorable first 30 seconds that grabs attention
4. **Story Structure** - Build three-act framework with narrative arc
5. **Evidence Integration** - Weave data, research, examples throughout
6. **Rhetorical Devices** - Add repetition, metaphors, rule of three
7. **Emotional Peaks** - Plan moments of inspiration, humor, reflection
8. **Call to Action** - End with clear challenge or invitation
9. **Memorable Close** - Callback to opening, powerful final line

### Workflow 2: Business Presentation
1. **Objective Clarity** - Define what you want audience to do/decide
2. **Situation Setup** - Establish context and why this matters now
3. **Problem Definition** - Articulate challenge or opportunity clearly
4. **Solution Presentation** - Explain your recommendation with rationale
5. **Benefit Articulation** - Connect solution to audience's priorities
6. **Objection Handling** - Address potential concerns proactively
7. **Action Steps** - Provide clear next steps and timeline
8. **Q&A Preparation** - Anticipate questions, prepare responses

### Workflow 3: Special Occasion Speech
1. **Occasion Understanding** - Grasp tone, expectations, time constraints
2. **Personal Connection** - Establish your relationship to subject/event
3. **Anecdote Selection** - Choose revealing, appropriate stories
4. **Emotional Balance** - Mix humor, sentiment, celebration appropriately
5. **Tribute & Thanks** - Acknowledge key people authentically
6. **Universal Themes** - Connect specific to broader human experience
7. **Toast/Challenge** - End with raise-your-glass or call-to-action moment

### Workflow 4: Persuasive Speech
1. **Position Statement** - Clearly state your argument
2. **Credibility Building** - Establish why audience should trust you
3. **Problem Amplification** - Make status quo feel unacceptable
4. **Solution Advocacy** - Present your position as best path forward
5. **Evidence Marshaling** - Use logos (logic), pathos (emotion), ethos (credibility)
6. **Counterargument Refutation** - Address opposing views respectfully
7. **Urgency Creation** - Explain why action needed now
8. **Inspiring Close** - Paint vision of better future

## Best Practices

- **Know your audience** - Speak to their values, concerns, aspirations
- **One core message** - If they remember nothing else, what should it be?
- **Open strong** - First 30 seconds determine if they'll really listen
- **Use the rule of three** - Points, stories, examples in threes stick
- **Tell stories** - Narratives persuade better than data alone
- **Write for the ear** - Use short sentences, conversational language
- **Create rhythm** - Vary sentence length, use repetition strategically
- **Plant callbacks** - Reference earlier points to create cohesion
- **Show, don't tell** - Vivid details over abstract concepts
- **Use active voice** - "We will build" not "It will be built"
- **Include pauses** - Mark [PAUSE] where speaker should breathe, let moment land
- **Build to crescendo** - Increase energy and stakes as you progress
- **Make it personal** - Share your connection to the topic
- **Practice-friendly** - Easy to read aloud, natural delivery
- **Time it** - Know your word count per minute (typically 120-150 words)
- **End memorably** - Last lines should echo after you leave stage
- **Cut ruthlessly** - Every word must earn its place
- **Read aloud** - If it sounds awkward spoken, rewrite it
- **Avoid jargon** - Unless audience is highly specialized
- **Include staging notes** - [SHOW SLIDE], [GESTURE], [PAUSE FOR LAUGH]
